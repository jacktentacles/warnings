Paracetamol

Paracetamol is a common painkiller used to treat aches and pain. It can also be used to reduce a high temperature (fever).

Call 999 now if:
  • You or someone you care for has difficulty breathing.
  • You experience a severe allergic reaction.
Speak to your doctor if:
  • You have liver or kidney problems.
  • You regularly drink large amounts of alcohol.
Important:
  • Do not take more than the recommended dose.
  • Always read the label or leaflet that comes with your medicine.

About Paracetamol

Paracetamol is used to relieve mild to moderate pain and reduce fever. It is suitable for most people but should be taken as directed.

Who Can Take Paracetamol

Most adults and children over 2 months old can take paracetamol. Check with a pharmacist or doctor if you're unsure.

How and When to Take It

Follow the instructions on the packaging or from your healthcare professional. Do not exceed the recommended dose.

Side Effects

Side effects from paracetamol are rare but can include allergic reactions and liver damage from overdose.

Pregnancy and Breastfeeding

Paracetamol is generally safe to take during pregnancy and breastfeeding, but you should consult with your doctor first.
